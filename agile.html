Agile Interview Questions

Agile Methodology

1. What is Agile?
Agile is a software development approach that focuses on iterative development, continuous feedback, fast delivery, and customer collaboration.
2. Core Agile Concepts
• Iterative Development
• Incremental Delivery
• Continuous Feedback
• Adaptability to Change
3. What is Scrum?
Scrum is an Agile framework used to develop, deliver, and maintain complex products using sprints, roles, and ceremonies.
4. What are the roles in Scrum?
1. Product Owner - Manages product backlog and priorities.
2. Scrum Master - Facilitates scrum process and removes blockers.
3. Development Team - Builds and delivers the product.
5. What is a Sprint?
A Sprint is a short time period (2-4 weeks) where a small set of work is completed.
6. Important Scrum Meetings
• Sprint Planning
• Daily Stand-up
• Sprint Review
• Sprint Retrospective
• Backlog Refinement
7. What is Product Backlog?
Product Backlog is a prioritized list of features, requirements, and tasks maintained by the Product Owner.
8. What is Sprint Backlog?
Sprint Backlog is the list of tasks selected from the product backlog for execution in the current sprint.
9. What is Daily Stand-up Meeting?
A 15-minute daily meeting where team members discuss what they did yesterday, what they will do today, and any blockers.
10. What are Scrum ceremonies?
Sprint Planning, Daily Stand-up, Sprint Review, and Sprint Retrospective.
11. What is Sprint Planning?
Sprint Planning is a meeting where the team defines the sprint goal and selects backlog items for the sprint.
12. What is Sprint Review?
Sprint Review is a meeting where the completed work is demonstrated to stakeholders and feedback is collected.
13. What is Sprint Retrospective?
A meeting where the team discusses what went well and how to improve in the next sprint.
14. What is Increment?
Increment is the sum of all completed product backlog items at the end of a sprint, representing working software.
15. What is Velocity?
Velocity is the amount of work completed by the team in one sprint, measured using story points.
16. What is Story Point?
Story point is a unit used to estimate the effort, complexity, and risk involved in completing a task.
17. What is the difference between Agile and Waterfall?
Agile is iterative and flexible, while Waterfall is sequential and rigid. Agile supports frequent changes and continuous delivery.
18. How do you handle requirement changes in Agile?
Requirement changes are added to the product backlog and prioritized for upcoming sprints.
19. What is your role in an Agile project as a developer?
To develop features, write unit tests, fix bugs, participate in sprint meetings, and continuously improve product quality.
20. What is Definition of Done (DoD)?
Definition of Done is a checklist that defines when a task or user story is considered complete.
21. What is a Burndown Chart?
A burndown chart is a graphical representation showing remaining work versus time in a sprint.

CI → Continuous Integration
